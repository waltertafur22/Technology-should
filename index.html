🔎 The Rule

We use should and shouldn't to give advice or say what is a good/bad idea.

should + verb

You should charge your laptop.
People should take breaks from screens.

shouldn't (should not) + verb

You shouldn't spend five hours playing games.
You shouldn't use your phone in class.

Common mistake: don't add "to" — say you should charge it, not you should to charge it. The verb after should never changes for he/she/it either: he should charge it, not he shoulds charge it.
